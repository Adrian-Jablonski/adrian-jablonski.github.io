About Me

Before Software Development

Education

I graduated from Montclair State University in 2014 with a Bachelor's degree in Accounting and a minor in Mathematics. During my last year of college, I was working as a Business Analyst intern for the IT department of Diversified Global Graphics Group (DG3). Upon graduation, I was offered and accepted a job in the finance department of DG3 as a Financial Analyst.

Work Experience

As a Financial Analyst I spent most of my time either updating reports or creating new reports using Microsoft Excel. I soon discovered my talent in being able to create reports quickly and efficiently and started to improve every report that I worked on. I learned how to create macros in Visual Basics for Applications and how to use Microsoft Access databases to save time on time consuming reports. After leaving DG3 for another position, I was asked to stay on at DG3 as a consultant and to this day I continue to help with automating manual procedures.


My next full time position was as a Pricing Analyst at Wurth USA. Right away I started to improve every Excel report that was given to me. This company also used Tableau for reports which was a completely new program for me. However, within the first month of using Tableau, I managed to build a dashboard in Tableau which automatically showed the effects of switching customers to different pricing programs. This process that was previously done manually in Excel, was now done by selecting a customer number and program in Tableau and clicking a button.

Software Development

My favorite part of my previous two jobs was being able to work with code to automate procedures and I realized that I wanted coding to be a larger part of my job. So I started teaching myself coding by reading books and using freecodecamp. After a few months of self teaching, I left my job at Wurth and moved to Houston, TX where I enrolled in Digital Crafts. Digital Crafts is a 16 week accelerated coding bootcamp for software engineers and full stack web development which covers topics such as HTML, JavaScript, and Python.
