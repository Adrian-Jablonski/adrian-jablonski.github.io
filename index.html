Education

I graduated from Montclair State University in 2014 with a Bachelor's degree in Accounting and a minor in Mathematics. During my last year of college, I was a Business Analyst intern for the IT department of a printing company called Diversified Global Graphics Group (DG3). Upon graduation, I accepted an offer in the Finance department as a Financial Analyst.

As a Financial Analyst, my main responsibilities were creating month end reports and reports for management. I soon discovered my ability to streamline the reporting process using Visual Basic, which started with automating simple Excel reports and grew to creating reporting solutions using VBA and Microsoft Access databases. I quickly became the go-to financial analyst for company management and performed onsite due diligence for three companies that DG3 acquired. Upon leaving DG3 for another opportunity, I was retained by DG3 for further projects as a part-time consultant, where I continue working to this day.


My next full time position was as a Pricing Analyst at Wurth USA, a global auto distributor. This was a new environment where I was able to apply and build on my previous knowledge to solve challenging problems which included using Tableau, which was a new application for me. Within the first month of using Tableau, I took a process which was previously done manually in Excel and built a dashboard in Tableau which automatically showed the effects of switching customers to different pricing programs and resulted in significant time savings for my department.

I realized that the programming aspects of my various experiences was what I was most attuned to. Wanting to develop that further, I started teaching myself coding by reading books and using freecodecamp. After a few months of self teaching, I realized this was my calling and started learning coding full time at Digital Crafts, a 16 week accelerated coding bootcamp for software engineers and full stack web developers.
